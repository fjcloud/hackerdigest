## Top Stories

### [Claude Code sends 33k tokens before reading the prompt; OpenCode sends 7k](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48883275)
A measurement post claiming Claude Code's pre-prompt payload is roughly 5x OpenCode's, with most of the bloat in tool schemas and skills. The thread surfaces the practical consensus: cache reads mostly neutralize the per-token cost, but Anthropic builds the harness, so it controls both the payload and the cache, and "Anthropic is a token merchant" (`korrectional`, `claw-el`) is the sharpest framing. `mcv`'s subagent example (7 spawned agents burning the whole 5-hour window before any work completes) is the single most actionable number. The author `systima` acknowledged the headline metric alone is incomplete and committed to publishing a full task-completion diff.

### [I love LLMs, I hate hype](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48883343)
geohot's third major LLM post, walking back his May "Eternal Sloptember" maximalism. The framing is that frontier-lab value capture is shaky because LLMs are mostly a product of Moore's-law-style compute progress, and the SF/Twitter-cycle FOMO around them is a manufactured sentiment. The thread is split between the model-builders (he's a merchant himself per `pphysch`), the skeptics who want the robots to stop, and the pragmatists. `SwellJoe`'s take -- that frontier labs need to push people onto usage-based billing to justify valuations, and OpenAI's decision to include GPT 5.6 Sol on the $20 sub is the real pressure point -- is the sharpest thread on the economic story.

### [Ask HN: Add flag for AI-generated articles](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48886741)
`dang`'s response is the actual story: he confirms the existing ban on LLM-generated comments, outlines plans for a flag-reason dropdown ("because I think it's genai" as one of several choices), and frames the community norm as "a class distinction between writes and write-nots" that emerges from a learned allergic reaction to LLM voice. He also commits to keeping tagging-as-just-an-indicator on the table. `minimaxir`'s warning -- false positives destroy authors who can't prove the negative -- is the best counter-take. `retconoffset`'s observation that coding-RLHF is actively making models *worse* at sounding human is the most useful single datapoint in the thread.

### [Migrating a production AI agent to GPT-5.6: 2.2x faster, 27% cheaper](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48882716)
Ploy's writeup is mostly the substance the AI-generated-looking prose hides: a real tool-calling rework at the provider boundary (optional properties rewritten as `anyOf: [T, null]`) plus an unannounced breaking change in OpenAI's prompt caching (partial-prefix matching gone). `lcampbell` flags that this looks more like a JSON-schema translation bug than a model-level fix; `dannyw`'s deeper observation is that the constrained-decoding-vs-intelligence tradeoff is a deliberate one Claude Code has decided is not worth taking on agentic loops. The "model + harness + prompt = one system, not modular" reading from `znnajdla` is the right framing for anyone tempted to do "easy" provider swaps.

### [Vint Cerf, "father of the Internet," is retiring](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48854168)

Best comments are the personal ones: `jibal`'s story of ARPANET-era Jotto and Cerf walking off in disgust upon learning the "AI" was just random-word elimination; `jrockway`'s account of Cerf picking up an internal essay and forcing leadership to take IPv6 seriously; `PaulHoule`'s contrarian take (he's less of a saint given ACM's H-1B positioning). `fragmede`'s list of the actual eleven "fathers of the Internet" is the canonical reference for next time someone invokes the singular.

## Projects & Tools

- **[Ghostel.el](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48879504)** -- Terminal emulator for Emacs powered by libghostty-vt, so the terminal buffer behaves like a normal Emacs buffer (kbd/mouse/links/search all work). Five input modes (semi-char / char / copy / emacs / line) replace vterm's two. Maintainers `dakra` and `baokaola` in-thread and actively filing issues; the supply-chain critique about the auto-downloaded native module is real but consistent with how vterm/jinx ship.
- **[Tiny Emulators](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48884395)** -- `floooh`'s long-running pin-level 8-bit emulator set (ZX Spectrum, C64, Atari, Amstrad) running in the browser. The pin-level interface is the interesting design call -- it's overkill for runtime but invaluable as an oracle for correctness verification, which `Lerc`'s comment generalizes into a wider argument for explicit behavioral interfaces in an AI-coded world.
- **[LARP](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48882569)** -- Satire of the AI-era circular-vendor-financing pattern (Nvidia/Anthropic/OpenAI diagrammed as the canonical case). Several commenters noted they couldn't tell it was a joke until the pricing page; that's the joke. Not a real tool.

## Worth Reading

- **[Mechanistic interpretability researchers applying causality theory to LLMs](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48883090)** -- CACM piece on using weight/activation interventions (rather than behavior probes) to test whether neural representations encode coherent concepts. The example that lands: one model uses the same machinery for clock-time and calendar-month arithmetic, hinting at a generalized "cyclical measure" feature. `dang` renames the submission to reflect that this is about mech-interp, not "reasoning" in the philosophical sense.
- **[Automation Without Understanding](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48882554)** -- arXiv essay arguing mathematical capacity should be treated as a strategic asset on par with semiconductor capability. The provocative claim is that handing cognition to AI while starving the pipeline of humans who can verify it is the actual civilizational risk, not the AI capability itself. `mondrian`'s reframe ("the singularity isn't AI racing forward, it's us being pushed back past the threshold where computers are legible") is the more durable line.
