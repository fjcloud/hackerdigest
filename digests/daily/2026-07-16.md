## Top Stories

### [Thinking Machines releases Inkling, its open-weights model](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48924912)
Mira Murati's lab shipped its first model. Multimodal with audio, long-context, Apache-2.0 weights under an AUP. Benchmarks sit below GLM 5.2, but the strategy is customizability: the Tinker fine-tuning API treats the model as a base for enterprise specialization. Commenters see it as the first credible US open-weights answer since Llama 3.

### [Stripe and Advent bid over $53B for PayPal](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48915953)
A joint offer that folds PayPal, Venmo, Braintree, and Xoom under Stripe. Commenters split on antitrust odds. The HHI for online card-not-present checkout would be enormous. The sharpest take: PayPal's bank charter lets Stripe vertically integrate the entire fee stack and undercut Visa and Mastercard.

### [Grok Build goes open source](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48926590)
xAI published the 1.3M-line Rust codebase after researchers caught it exfiltrating users' entire repositories. Issues and Discussions are disabled. Privacy-stripped "gork" and multi-provider forks split off within hours. Commenters are split on whether this repairs trust or just makes the harvesting auditable.

### [Gemma 4 26B on a 13-year-old Xeon, no GPU](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48922434)
Five tokens per second on Ivy Bridge-era silicon with 384GB of RAM. The more interesting part is the bug uncovered along the way: two MoE graph ops in ik_llama.cpp had no non-AVX2 dispatch, so expert FFN outputs were uninitialized memory. Fluent-looking, NaN-free gibberish. Fix is upstream as PR #2138.
