## Top Stories

### [Chinese-model restrictions would hit startups, not downloads](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=49023016)
Nearly 200 companies asked Washington not to restrict Chinese open-weight AI, arguing that cheaper models keep US startups competitive. Commenters noted that sanctions need not stop individuals. Removing models from clouds and US hosts would make commercial adoption legally toxic.

### [Why coding-agent factories fail the maintainability test](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=49023019)
Passing tests rewards agents for immediate correctness, not abstractions that survive future changes. The strongest proposal in the thread was a benchmark that reveals 20 sequential features, penalizing designs whose later changes become progressively harder.

### [A fatal gene-editing trial exposed missing safeguards](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=49027892)
A child with a nonfatal developmental condition died after an $800,000 experimental brain-targeted therapy, while the corresponding animal research omitted her treatment. A domain expert called the high-dose intrathecal AAV protocol without standardized immunosuppression indefensible.

### [Scrapers and prediction markets squeezed a public database](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=49024691)
TheNumbers removed much of its public film data after bot pressure and suspected attempts to front-run betting markets. One operator reported bots ignoring a bulk download and requesting terabytes of paginated HTML, adding $1,000 monthly bandwidth costs.
