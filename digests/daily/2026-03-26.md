## Top Stories

### [Thoughts on slowing the fuck down](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47517539)
Mario Zechner, creator of the Pi coding agent, argues that AI-driven development is turning software into a brittle, unmaintainable mess because developers are prioritizing speed over architecture. The discussion highlights a critical insight: the real output of programming isn't just the code, but the programmer's mental model of the system, which is lost when agents do all the typing.

### [The EU still wants to scan your private messages and photos](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47522709)
The controversial "Chat Control" mass surveillance bill was brought back for another vote in the EU Parliament. In a narrow 307-306 decision, the push for indiscriminate scanning was defeated, replaced by a requirement for targeted scanning with judicial oversight. The thread is highly critical of the political tactic of repeatedly forcing votes on unpopular surveillance measures until they pass.

### [ARC-AGI-3](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47521150)
François Chollet released the third iteration of his ARC-AGI benchmark, introducing interactive, multi-turn visual reasoning puzzles. Frontier LLMs currently score near zero. Chollet appeared in the comments to defend the strict "no human-crafted harness" rule, arguing that true general intelligence should not require hand-holding to adapt to novel environments.
