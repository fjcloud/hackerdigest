### [Migrating from DigitalOcean to Hetzner](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47815774)
A developer migrated their infrastructure to a Hetzner dedicated server, dropping their monthly bill from $1,432 to $233. The HN discussion is a classic debate on the hidden costs of bare metal vs. cloud: you save 80% on compute, but you lose live migrations, redundant PSUs, and managed backups. As one commenter noted, "Anyone who thinks DO and Hetzner dedicated servers are fungible products is making a mistake."

### [Anonymous request-token comparisons from Opus 4.6 and Opus 4.7](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47816960)
Anthropic's new Opus 4.7 model uses a new tokenizer that consumes up to 45% more input tokens for the same prompt compared to 4.6. Since pricing per token remains the same, this effectively acts as a massive price hike for input-heavy workflows like coding agents. The HN thread is full of frustrated developers hitting their usage limits in hours, with many suspecting the change is a deliberate move to improve unit economics.

### [Why Japan has such good railways](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47815395)
An analysis of Japan's railway success points to a unique model: private rail companies act as "city-shaping" real estate developers, building retail and housing around their stations to capture the value their transit creates. The comments highlight that this is enabled by Japan's liberal zoning laws and federalized planning, making it nearly impossible to replicate in the US where local NIMBYism and strict zoning block transit-oriented development.
