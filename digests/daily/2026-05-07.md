## Top Stories

### [Valve releases Steam Controller CAD files under Creative Commons license](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48037555)
Valve released the CAD files for the Steam Controller's external shell under a CC BY-NC-SA license, encouraging users to 3D print mods and replacements. The community praised Valve's hardware preservation ethos, noting how valuable this is for disabled gamers who need to build custom accessibility adapters.

### [Google Cloud fraud defense, the next evolution of reCAPTCHA](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48039362)
Google is introducing a QR code-based reCAPTCHA challenge that requires users to scan it with a mobile device to prove "humanness." HN is outraged, viewing this as a hostile move that breaks web anonymity, punishes desktop and dumbphone users, and forces reliance on the Google/Apple mobile duopoly.

### [Vibe coding and agentic engineering are getting closer than I'd like](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48037128)
Simon Willison explores the tension of AI generating 2,000 lines of code a day in an industry where the review lifecycle was designed for 200 lines. The comments debate the fallout: while some fear a future of unmaintainable "slop," others argue we must adapt by shifting our focus entirely to architecture and property-based testing.
