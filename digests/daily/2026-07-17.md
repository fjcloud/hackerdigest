## Top Stories

### [Kimi K3: Open Frontier Intelligence](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48935342)
Moonshot ships a 2.8T-parameter MoE claiming to trail only Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 Sol. Weights promised by July 27. Pricing matches Anthropic at $3/$15 per million tokens, a sharp break from the subsidized era. Commenters caught the awkward phrasing -- "ranks second only to two others" means third. Early testers report the model is strong but subscription quotas are brutal.

### [Microsoft Comic Chat is now open source](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48936426)
The 1996 IRC client that rendered chat as comics lands on GitHub. Vintage C++ and MFC, plus a few AI-assisted modernization patches to get it building in current Visual Studio. The original developer endorsed the release, which took six years to clear.

### [How Our Rust-to-Zig Rewrite Is Going](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48933149)
Richard Feldman explains why the Roc compiler team left Rust for Zig. Headline number: 35ms incremental rebuilds. The thread pushes back on memory-safety claims. One Zig-experienced commenter noted ReleaseSafe does not catch use-after-free as the post implies.

### [Decoy Font](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48936584)
A font where humans squint to see one message and vision models confidently read the other. Several commenters posted one-line ImageMagick recipes that recover the "hidden" text in seconds. The security premise is already broken. Still fun as art.
