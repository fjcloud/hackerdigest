## Top Stories

### [Gemma 4 runs locally on iPhone with tool use](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47652561)
Google released a demo app that runs the new Gemma 4 (E2B and E4B) models entirely on-device for iOS and Android. The models support agentic skills and mobile actions like controlling the flashlight or opening maps. The HN thread highlights the impressive inference speed on recent hardware (up to 30 tokens/sec) and notes that capable, local models are finally becoming practical for daily use without cloud dependencies.

### [Microsoft hasn't had a coherent GUI strategy since Petzold](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47651703)
A critique of Microsoft's chaotic history with desktop UI frameworks, tracking the churn from Win32 and WinForms to WPF, UWP, and WinUI. The consensus in the comments is that the constant deprecation cycle has driven developers away from native Windows development entirely. Many report defaulting to web wrappers like Electron or sticking with decades-old WinForms because it remains stable.

### [Why Switzerland has 25 Gbit internet and America doesn't](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47652400)
An analysis of the Swiss broadband model, where multiple physical fibers are run to each home and ISPs compete at the central office layer, contrasting with the US model of local infrastructure monopolies. The discussion debates whether the US telecom sector can even be called a free market given its regulatory capture, while others question the practical utility of 25 Gbps for residential users.

### [Employers use your personal data to figure out the lowest salary you'll accept](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47655466)
Companies are increasingly using services like Equifax's "The Work Number" to access candidates' past salary histories and optimize lowball offers. The HN thread serves as a PSA on how to freeze your data on these platforms, highlighting the severe information asymmetry in modern salary negotiations where employers know your financial history but you don't know their budget.
