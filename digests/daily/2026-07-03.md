### [Virginia bans sale of geolocation data](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48767347)
Virginia has enacted a law banning commercial sale of geolocation data collected from state residents, one of the first such targeted restrictions at the state level. The thread's central debate was jurisdictional arbitrage: a Delaware-incorporated company with no Virginia operations but Virginia-sourced data sits in murky legal territory, and readers expected most vendors to make a practical judgment call rather than fully restructure their data pipelines.

### [Since Linux 6.9, LUKS suspend stopped wiping disk-encryption keys from memory](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48763035)
A regression introduced in Linux 6.9 causes `cryptsetup luksSuspend` to skip the memory wipe of disk-encryption keys during suspend, leaving them accessible in RAM on sleeping systems. The subcommand is absent from stock configurations, but Debian and several other distributions ship the wrapper tooling that depends on it; the vulnerability affects any setup that uses `luksSuspend` for suspend-to-RAM security.

### [Podman v6.0.0](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48762098)
Red Hat's daemonless container runtime ships 6.0.0 with updated networking and improved rootless support. The 196-comment thread returned to the perennial question of why Docker remains dominant despite Podman's architectural advantages: the fault line is compose compatibility, with Quadlets defenders calling them underrated and critics arguing the multi-file, systemd-bound setup is a genuine portability regression for anyone not operating Linux-first.
