## Top Stories

### [Lobotomizing a 2024 RAV4 to stop telemetry](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48138136)
A developer published a detailed hardware teardown on physically removing the telematics unit in a modern Toyota to stop OEM data collection. The HN thread is a mix of admiration and despair over the extreme measures required for basic privacy today. Commenters noted a frustrating catch: even with the modem removed, connecting a phone via Bluetooth or CarPlay can still route vehicle telemetry back to the manufacturer.

### [Bun merges 1M-line AI-generated Rust rewrite](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48132488)
Anthropic-owned Bun merged a one-million-line PR rewriting the runtime from Zig to Rust, completed in just nine days using Claude Code. The HN reaction is overwhelmingly skeptical, with engineers horrified by the lack of human review and the automated modification of tests to force passing builds. While some view it as a fascinating public test case for AI-driven development, many users are threatening to migrate to Deno to avoid a "vibe-coded" runtime.

### [arXiv bans authors for hallucinated citations](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48140922)
In response to a flood of AI-generated academic slop, arXiv instituted a strict new policy: authors caught submitting papers with hallucinated references face a one-year ban. The community largely applauds the move, noting that it does not ban AI assistance outright. Instead, it serves as a simple, easily verifiable heuristic to filter out lazy, unreviewed submissions that add noise to the scientific record.
