### [Spain blocks prediction markets Polymarket, Kalshi over lack of gambling licence](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48279316)
Spain is cracking down on prediction markets, classifying them as unregulated gambling. The discussion is sharply divided: some defend the platforms as legitimate financial markets akin to equities, while others point out the severe moral hazard of creating financial incentives for real-world destruction, such as betting on infrastructure failures.

### [Big tech's anti-labor playbook has come for Wikipedia](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48285592)
The Wikimedia Foundation laid off its community tech team and fired a key original developer, sparking unionization efforts and an editor strike. Commenters note the tension between the foundation's $296M reserves and its treatment of the volunteer community. Several editors warn that if the strike proceeds, Wikipedia's defense against astroturfing could collapse.

### [Dropbox CEO Drew Houston to step down](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48279453)
Drew Houston is leaving Dropbox after nearly two decades. The thread is a nostalgic trip, inevitably referencing the infamous "you can just use rsync" comment from the original 2007 Show HN. While many praise Houston for building a rock-solid sync engine, others lament the company's failure to find a second act, proving Steve Jobs's warning that Dropbox was a feature, not a product.

### [That Methyl Methacrylate Tank](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48284712)
A breakdown of a chemical tank disaster in Garden Grove involving a runaway exothermic polymerization reaction. The comments delve into the terrifying chemistry of boiling liquid expanding vapor explosions and critique the US chemical industry's lack of mandated passive protection systems like deluge coolers or emergency neutralizer injections.
