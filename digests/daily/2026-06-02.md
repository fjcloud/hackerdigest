### [The newest Instagram “exploit” is the goofiest I've seen](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48359102)
A zero-auth password reset exploit allowed hackers to take over Instagram accounts by simply asking Meta's AI support bot to send verification codes to an arbitrary email address. The incident highlights the danger of giving LLMs privileged access to account recovery flows. Commenters suspect this was a failure of giving an AI agent access to an internal tool meant for human support reps, bypassing standard security checks.

### [OpenAI frontier models and Codex are now available on AWS](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48363132)
OpenAI's models are finally available on AWS Bedrock, breaking Anthropic's monopoly on the platform and Microsoft's exclusivity with OpenAI. Enterprise users in the thread note this is a massive shift, as many large companies have strict data governance that prevents them from using new vendors, making Bedrock the only approved path for AI integration.

### [Debug Project](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48362347)
A project (formerly under Verily, now Google) is expanding its efforts to release sterile mosquitoes in California and Florida to crash populations of disease-carrying invasive species. Discussion centers on the ecological impact, with consensus that the target species (*Aedes aegypti*) is invasive and its removal would likely just restore the prior ecosystem balance without harming native predators.
