## Top Stories

### [Dirtyfrag: Universal Linux LPE](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48053623)
A new universal Linux Local Privilege Escalation vulnerability called Dirtyfrag has been disclosed after an embargo was broken. Similar to the recent Copy Fail exploit, it relies on page cache poisoning via obscure socket address families. Because the embargo failed, proof-of-concept exploits are circulating before major distributions have released patches. The community is debating the viability of responsible disclosure in the LLM era, where public patches are instantly reverse-engineered into weaponized exploits.

### [Cloudflare to cut about 20% workforce](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48054423)
Cloudflare is laying off over 1,100 employees, with the CEO citing a 600% increase in internal AI usage and the need to "architect our company for the agentic AI era." HN commenters are highly skeptical of the AI productivity narrative, pointing instead to Cloudflare's deteriorating gross margins and recent earnings miss. Many view "AI efficiency" as a convenient, investor-friendly scapegoat for standard corporate cost-cutting.

### [Chrome removes claim of On-device AI not sending data to Google Servers](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48050964)
Following yesterday's revelation that Chrome is silently downloading a 4GB Gemini Nano model to users' devices, Google has quietly removed language claiming that its on-device AI doesn't send data to their servers. The discussion reflects deep cynicism, with users suspecting that local AI is being used to pre-process user behavior before exfiltrating telemetry to Google, further driving the push toward alternative browsers.
