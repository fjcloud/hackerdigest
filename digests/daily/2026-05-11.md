## Top Stories

### [Hardware Attestation as Monopoly Enabler](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48086190)
GrapheneOS developers argue that hardware attestation APIs (like Google Play Integrity) are being used to lock out alternative operating systems under the guise of security. The thread highlights how these checks are increasingly mandated by banks and governments, effectively forcing users into the Apple/Google duopoly. Commenters note the irony of using asymmetric cryptography—originally a tool for decentralization—to enforce centralized control.

### [Local AI needs to be the norm](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48085821)
A push for running smaller, specialized AI models locally rather than relying on cloud APIs for every feature. The author argues that while local models aren't as smart, they excel at constrained tasks like summarization and extraction. The HN discussion is divided: some see local inference as essential for privacy and cost-control, while others point out that the hardware requirements (32GB+ RAM) remain prohibitive for mainstream users.

### [I returned to AWS and was reminded why I left](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48073201)
A developer's frustrated account of returning to AWS and finding it overly complex, expensive, and filled with billing footguns compared to modern alternatives like Cloudflare or Hetzner. The comments section is a goldmine of cloud infrastructure debates, with many agreeing that AWS has become a "day-2" enterprise cash cow, while defenders argue that its core services (EC2, S3, IAM) remain unmatched for reliability at scale.

### [Incident Report: CVE-2024-YIKES](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48086082)
A highly realistic, satirical incident report about a cascading supply-chain attack involving a compromised npm package, a dog eating a YubiKey, and AI-generated code. It perfectly captures the fragility of modern software dependencies. Several commenters admitted they thought it was real until halfway through, highlighting how absurd actual supply-chain vulnerabilities have become.
