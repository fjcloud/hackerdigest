## Top Stories

### [Anthropic and OpenAI's path to profitability](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48296794)
Simon Willison argues that frontier AI labs have found a sustainable business model in coding agents, with enterprise customers paying API prices for heavy token usage. The HN comments are highly skeptical of the trillion-dollar valuations, noting that open-weight models like DeepSeek and GLM-5.1 are now capable enough for coding tasks at a fraction of the cost.

### [DuckDuckGo traffic spikes following Google AI Overviews](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48296649)
Following Google's aggressive rollout of AI Overviews, DuckDuckGo experienced a significant traffic increase. While some users admit to finding Google's AI mode useful for quick answers, the consensus is that the lack of an off-switch and the degradation of traditional search results are driving users to alternatives.

### [YouTube to automatically label AI-generated videos](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48299753)
YouTube will begin automatically applying labels to videos that its systems detect as having significant photorealistic AI use. The community is deeply skeptical about the reliability of AI detectors, fearing false positives that could hurt creators. The most requested feature in the comments isn't labeling, but a simple checkbox to filter AI content out of recommendations entirely.

### [GitHub suffers another major outage](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48293080)
GitHub suffered another major outage affecting pull requests and API operations, continuing a string of recent reliability issues. The discussion highlighted growing frustration with the platform's stability post-Microsoft acquisition. Several engineers speculated that the massive surge in automated commits and API calls from AI coding agents is effectively stressing infrastructure that wasn't built for agent scale.
