## Top Stories

### [Someone bought 30 WordPress plugins and planted a backdoor in all of them](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47755629)
An attacker purchased 30 established WordPress plugins on Flippa and injected backdoors to serve SEO spam and malicious redirects. The HN discussion points out that this isn't a technical hack but an economic one—buying trust and distribution is far easier and cheaper than finding zero-days, highlighting a structural flaw in ecosystems where updates are treated as implicit trust signals.

### [GitHub Stacked PRs](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47757495)
GitHub has introduced native support for Stacked PRs (currently in private preview via a CLI tool), bringing the Phabricator/Gerrit workflow to the platform. It allows developers to break large changes into smaller, independently reviewable PRs that build on each other. Commenters note the implementation currently relies heavily on creating new branches per change, unlike native stacking tools like Jujutsu.

### [DaVinci Resolve – Photo](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47760529)
Blackmagic Design announced a photo editing mode for DaVinci Resolve, bringing its advanced color grading engine to still images. Photographers welcome a viable, subscription-free alternative to Adobe Lightroom that also works natively on Linux. However, early testers note the UI still feels very much like a video editor with photo features tacked on.

### [Nothing Ever Happens: Polymarket bot that always buys No on non-sports markets](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47753472)
A developer created a bot that automatically bets "No" on non-sports prediction markets, exploiting the fact that roughly 73% of Polymarket bets resolve to "No." The thread features a deep discussion on market microstructure, noting that humans consistently overprice dramatic outcomes because reality is usually boring.

### [US appeals court declares 158-year-old home distilling ban unconstitutional](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47751781)
The 5th Circuit Court of Appeals struck down the federal ban on home distilling, a major win for hobbyists that challenges the broad application of the Interstate Commerce Clause. The comments thoroughly debunk the myth of methanol poisoning in home distilling, explaining that dangerous methanol levels almost exclusively come from adulterated industrial alcohol, not fruit or grain fermentation.
