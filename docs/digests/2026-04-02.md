### [LinkedIn Is Illegally Searching Your Computer](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47613981)
LinkedIn is using JavaScript to silently scan Chrome-based browsers for thousands of specific installed extensions, including job search tools and political/religious taggers. The HN discussion highlights how Chrome's extension API allows this fingerprinting, while Firefox users remain unaffected.

### [DRAM pricing is killing the hobbyist SBC market](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47606840)
The massive demand for high-margin HBM memory in AI data centers has caused commodity DRAM prices to skyrocket, pushing the 16GB Raspberry Pi 5 to $300. Commenters note that the hobbyist market is collateral damage, and many are abandoning SBCs in favor of cheap, refurbished corporate mini-PCs.

### [Steam on Linux Use Skyrocketed Above 5% in March](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47609564)
Linux usage on Steam has crossed the 5% threshold, driven by the Steam Deck's popularity and the continuous improvements in Proton. The HN thread is full of users who finally made the switch, citing Microsoft's aggressive push of AI features in Windows 11 as the final straw.
