## Top Stories

### [GPT-5.6 ships with Sol, Terra, and Luna tiers](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48849066)
OpenAI's GPT-5.6 family lands with three sizes (Sol $5/$30, Terra $2.50/$15, Luna $1/$6 per 1M tokens) and the most aggressive Fable-comparison marketing to date -- the release notes name-drop Fable 15 times. Headline claims: 53.6 on Agents' Last Exam (beating Fable 5 by 13.1), 80 on Artificial Analysis Coding Index with half the output tokens and one-third the cost. Sam Altman also confirmed Sol will run on Cerebras at 750 tok/sec. The HN mood is sharply split: `system2` leads with "they're just changing the decimals to stay relevant" and `enraged_camel` flags the custom "adaptive" vs "max" reasoning cherry-pick. OpenAI employee `tedsanders` shows up to explain the rollout pace. Most actionable comment: `minimaxir` highlights the developer-guide finding that shorter prompts improve scores 10-15% while cutting tokens 41-66%.

### [EU Parliament greenlights Chat Control 1.0](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48843923)
After rejecting the measure twice in March, the Parliament passed it today via the urgency procedure on the last session day before summer break. The procedural trick: 314 MEPs voted against, 276 in favor, 17 abstentions, but rejection needed an absolute majority of 361 votes, and 113 MEPs were absent. Law is in force until 2028. E2EE messaging (WhatsApp, Signal) is exempt for now; the scanned surface is direct messages on Instagram, Discord, Snapchat, Skype, Xbox plus Gmail/iCloud. The thread's sharpest comment: `spwa4`'s framing -- "have you tried getting a majority to not pass the law?" -- and `teekert`'s tally of the procedural absurdity. `ben_w` provides the only steelman for both sides.

### [Postgres rewritten in Rust passes 100% of the regression suite](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48841676)
`malisper`'s pgrust now passes the full Postgres regression suite after 7,101 commits in under a month. The interesting wrinkle in-thread: the GitHub version is 8x slower than Postgres, but the author claims an unpublished version hits 50% faster on transactional workloads and ~300x faster on analytical (2x slower than ClickHouse on clickbench). Strong skepticism from `evil-olive` and `tormeh`: 2,664 unsafe blocks and 1,835 unsafe fns, mostly in a parser generated mechanically via c2rust. The "rewrite it in Rust" meme fatigue is real -- `voihannena` links to a running collection. `jillesvangurp` lands the most balanced take: passing decades of curated tests is a real achievement even if new issues will surface.

### [Meta ships Muse Spark 1.1 at haiku prices](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48846184)
Meta's closed-weight Muse Spark 1.1 lands at $1.25/$4.50 per 1M tokens with $0.15 cached input -- undercutting Grok 4.5 ($2/$6, $0.50 cached). `Tiberium` and `ignoramous` both frame this as the second signal after Grok 4.5 that US labs feel the GLM 5.2 squeeze. The community isn't buying the benchmarks uncritically: `GodelNumbering` (ex-Meta) caught Meta overriding Terminal-Bench 2.1's per-task CPU/RAM caps, which disqualifies the published numbers. Not on OpenRouter, not in many regions, no weights -- the practical value depends entirely on trust, which `qpricjalcbeu` and `phillipcarter` both flagged.

### [Tencent's open-weight Hy3 quietly beats the closed US frontier on cost](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48847552)
Hy3 (295B params, MIT-licensed) has the cleanest cost-to-capability story in the open-weight world right now: matches DeepSeek V4 Pro on some benchmarks while costing the same as V4 Flash. `nmfisher`'s experience matches several others -- DS4 Flash is fast but loses the plot on longer context, Hy3 stays on track. The realistic counter from `wolttam`: Hy3 has no DSv4-style lightning indexer, so at FP4 you only get ~130K tokens of KV cache vs ~3M on a pair of DGX Sparks. Benchmarks for the preview were contaminated enough that OpenRouter rankings had dropped it to 8th/9th by today.

## Projects & Tools

- **[18 Words](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48845049)** -- Daily anagram game: 18 rounds, 30 seconds each, one missed word ends the run. Best community thread of the day for the actual builder-question sub-discussion.
