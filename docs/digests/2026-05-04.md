## Top Stories

### [Mercedes-Benz commits to bringing back physical buttons](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47997418)
Mercedes-Benz is reversing the industry trend of all-touchscreen car interiors, committing to bringing back tactile controls. The HN discussion is highly supportive, noting that touchscreens in cars are dangerous and lack necessary tactile feedback. However, some commenters speculate this reversal is driven more by upcoming EU NCAP safety ratings and Chinese regulations than by customer feedback alone.

### [OpenAI's o1 correctly diagnosed 67% of ER patients vs. 50-55% by triage doctors](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47991981)
A new study shows OpenAI's o1 model outperforming human doctors in diagnosing ER patients based on electronic health records. HN commenters quickly pointed out a major flaw in the methodology: both the AI and the doctors were given only text notes. Since doctors rely heavily on visual cues and patient interaction, this was effectively a test of "diagnosing from paperwork" rather than actual clinical practice.

### [Agentic Coding Is a Trap](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48002442)
An article arguing that relying heavily on AI agents for coding leads to skill atrophy, making developers less capable of understanding and reviewing the generated code. The thread sparked a deep debate about the nature of software engineering. Many agree that writing code builds the mental models needed to maintain it, while others argue that agentic coding is just a new abstraction layer, akin to moving from assembly to high-level languages.
