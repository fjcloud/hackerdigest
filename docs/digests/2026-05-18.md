## Top Stories

### [Mozilla to UK regulators: VPNs are essential privacy and security tools](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48166459)
Mozilla formally pushed back against a UK government consultation considering age-gating or restricting VPNs. The HN discussion highlights the paradox that the exact reason VPNs are essential—privacy—is why governments want to restrict them. Commenters view this as a broader erosion of digital rights under the guise of child safety, noting that age verification is effectively mass surveillance under a fake name.

### [I don't think AI will make your processes go faster](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48168221)
A blog post arguing that AI won't speed up software development in large organizations because the bottleneck is usually bureaucracy, requirements gathering, and communication, not typing code. The consensus in the comments is that while AI is a massive multiplier for solo developers or small teams, in large companies it often just generates more low-context output that requires extensive human review and reconciliation.

### [I turned a $80 RK3562 Android tablet into a Debian Linux workstation](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48168668)
A developer reverse-engineered a cheap Doogee Android tablet to boot Debian natively from an SD card without unlocking the bootloader or modifying internal storage. The project proves that modern mobile hardware is massively underutilized once vendor support ends, and simple external boot support could extend their life for homelabs or edge computing.
