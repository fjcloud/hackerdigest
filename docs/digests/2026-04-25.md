### [Google's $40B investment in Anthropic](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47892074)
Google is pouring $40 billion into Anthropic, a direct competitor to its own Gemini models. The HN discussion cuts through the headline, pointing out this is essentially massive-scale vendor financing. Anthropic is a major customer for Google's TPUs and cloud services, meaning a significant chunk of this capital will flow right back to Google's balance sheet while securing compute market share against Nvidia.

### [How overthinking kills engineering projects](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47890799)
A reflection on how engineers routinely sabotage their own work by over-researching prior art and expanding scope before shipping a v1. The thread is highly relatable, drawing parallels to the structural traps of PhD research and referencing the WWII-era CIA Simple Sabotage Field Manual to show how demanding perfection and deep analysis are classic tactics for organizational paralysis.

### [The search for a scientific theory of deep learning](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47893779)
A new paper argues that the empirical trial-and-error of deep learning architectures will eventually yield to a rigorous scientific theory, akin to statistical mechanics. The HN crowd is deeply skeptical. Commenters argue that the fundamental complexity of modern AI lies in the massive, unstructured training datasets rather than the model architectures, making a clean mathematical theory unlikely.
