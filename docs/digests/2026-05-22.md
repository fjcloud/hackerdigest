## Top Stories

### [Flipper One – we need your help](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48220647)
The creators of Flipper Zero announced Flipper One, a highly ambitious, open-source ARM Linux "cyberdeck" with extensive connectivity (Wi-Fi, cellular, SDR). They are asking the community to help reverse-engineer proprietary hardware blobs. The HN thread is split between enthusiasm for a truly hackable pocket computer and skepticism about massive scope creep, with hardware engineers noting the extreme difficulty of reverse-engineering RF and memory timing blobs.

### [Google's Antigravity bait and switch](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48222529)
Google abruptly replaced its Antigravity IDE (a VS Code fork with Gemini integration) with a purely conversational agent interface in version 2.0, breaking workflows and deleting local histories without warning. The incident sparked intense frustration over Google's habit of rug-pulling products. The discussion highlights a growing divide: some developers embrace the new "agentic" workflow where the AI writes everything, while others refuse to surrender their traditional IDEs and granular control.

### [We're testing new ad formats in Search](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48220105)
Google announced it is integrating ads directly into its AI-generated search overviews. The community saw this as the inevitable enshittification of AI search. Commenters pointed out the insidious nature of AI ads: unlike traditional search results, it is much harder to distinguish between an objective AI synthesis and a paid product placement seamlessly woven into the generated text.

### [Waymo pauses Atlanta service as its robotaxis keep driving into floods](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48225426)
Waymo temporarily halted its robotaxi service in Atlanta after multiple vehicles drove into flooded streets and got stuck. The incident underscores the limitations of current sensor fusion. Commenters debated whether this is a fundamental flaw in AI reasoning—an inability to judge water depth using visual context like a human—or just another edge case that will be solved by adding flooded roads to the simulation data.
