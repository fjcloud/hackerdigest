## Top Stories

### [A 1,000x faster LLM tokenizer](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=49010167)
GigaToken replaces regex-heavy pretokenization with SIMD, branch reduction, and an aggressive cache, reporting roughly 1,000x throughput over common APIs. Preliminary inference tests show the practical ceiling: 5-10% lower time-to-first-token, while training-data preprocessing may benefit far more.

### [Codeberg bans cryptocurrency projects](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=49015588)
The nonprofit forge voted to exclude cryptocurrency repositories, shortly after restricting predominantly AI-generated projects. The thread's useful lesson is operational, not ideological: Git is portable, but project identity and discovery are not, so maintain mirrors and a domain you control.

### [Reddit puts Old Reddit behind login](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=49005747)
Reddit is gradually requiring authentication for its lightweight old interface, citing scraper abuse. Commenters offered a plausible technical rationale: one JavaScript-heavy frontend exposes more behavioral signals for bot detection. Licensing data to selected AI vendors makes the access policy hard to separate from monetization.
