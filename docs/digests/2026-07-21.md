## Top Stories

### [Hacker wipes Romania's national land registry](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48978605)
An attacker using valid credentials wiped Romania's ANCPI database, including the network-reachable backups. The agency claims offline copies survived. Commenters savaged the recovery plan of migrating to Government Cloud as "fixing the mistake by repeating it." Slovakia's registry took the same hit in January 2025 and froze for a month.

### [China's open-weights AI strategy is winning](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48979269)
A long-form essay argues Chinese labs are winning the AI race by open-sourcing frontier models and undercutting US proprietary labs. The most concrete comment: one engineer runs two GPU clusters and reports Chinese models deliver about 1/20 the per-token cost. Startups use Chinese models for user-facing features, US models for dev work.

### [Human mathematicians are being outcounterexampled](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48983382)
Kevin Buzzard reflects on AI finding counterexamples humans missed for decades, including a degree-7 polynomial refuting the Jacobian Conjecture. The sharpest counter: mathematicians had already checked degree-100 polynomials in two variables. The pattern suggests the problem was under-explored rather than beyond human reach.
