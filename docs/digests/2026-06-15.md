## Top Stories

### [Curl will not accept vulnerability reports during July 2026](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48537165)
Daniel Stenberg announced the curl team is taking a month off and will ignore vulnerability reports from anyone without a paid support contract. A rare and fascinating approach to open-source burnout. The comments applaud the "Fuck You Pay Me" boundary-setting, highlighting the tension between corporate reliance on free software and maintainer well-being.

### [Rio de Janeiro's "homegrown" LLM appears to be a merge of an existing model](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48528371)
The municipality of Rio released a 397B parameter LLM, claiming it was a homegrown fine-tune of Qwen3.5. Researchers quickly discovered it's actually just a weighted merge of two existing open-weight models. A hilarious example of AI hype meeting government bureaucracy, though the thread contains a surprisingly deep discussion on why linear mode connectivity (merging weights) actually works.

### [How to earn a billion dollars](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48526360)
Paul Graham argues that becoming a billionaire doesn't require cheating; it's simply the mathematical result of compounding exponential growth. The HN thread is fiercely divided. Many commenters push back hard, arguing that maintaining such aggressive growth inevitably requires exploiting workers, skirting regulations, or monopolistic behavior.

## Projects & Tools

- **[Kage](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48529990)** -- A modern take on `httrack` that crawls JavaScript-heavy websites by capturing the DOM after rendering, then packages the entire site into a single executable binary for offline viewing. ([repo](https://github.com/tamnd/kage))
- **[Trace](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48521236)** -- A native Mac app for offline meeting transcription using local Whisper models, with a neat feature to flag important moments mid-call. ([site](https://traceapp.info))
- **[Zeroserve](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48527145)** -- An eBPF-based web server running in userspace that added Caddy compatibility, claiming massive performance gains. The comments are highly skeptical about the security implications of JIT-compiling eBPF for a web server. ([site](https://su3.io/posts/zeroserve-caddy-compat))
