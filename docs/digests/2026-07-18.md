## Top Stories

### [An AWS billing bug generated trillion-dollar estimates](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48945241)
AWS briefly showed absurd S3 estimates, from millions to trillions, after a unit-pricing bug in its billing computation subsystem. Alerts fired, but the incident banner was buried away from billing. One commenter noted that subtle 20% overcharges would have been harder to catch.

### [TP-Link cameras exposed previous owners’ home coordinates](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48952565)
TP-Link Kasa EC71 cameras answered an unauthenticated local-network UDP packet with sub-meter GPS coordinates and retained previous-owner data after factory reset. They also shared a fleet-wide RSA key and used unsalted MD5 credentials. Six months of disclosure produced a beta fix that bricked the researcher’s camera.

### [Mozilla says open models won. The data is less decisive](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48947825)
Mozilla reports open models now handle 63% of OpenRouter tokens, up from 40% four months ago, while inference costs fell roughly 50x. HN’s best objection is methodological: direct vendor APIs and subscriptions are absent from that sample. The report also blurs open weights with reproducibly open training.

### [AI is amplifying call-center management in healthcare](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48952880)
Kaiser nurses say scripted advice limits and surveillance-driven coaching punish the long calls that complex care requires. The thread usefully corrects the framing: the empathy-grading AI pilot ended in 2024, while conventional handle-time metrics remain the active dispute.
