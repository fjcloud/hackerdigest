## Top Stories

### [All elementary functions from a single binary operator](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47746610)
A new paper demonstrates that all elementary mathematical functions (exp, ln, sin, cos, etc.) can be derived from a single binary operator: `eml(x, y) = exp(x) - ln(y)`. The HN thread highlights the theoretical elegance—comparing it to NAND gates in boolean logic—while debating its practical utility for hardware acceleration or symbolic regression.

### [Apple's accidental moat: How the "AI Loser" may end up winning](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47747017)
An argument that Apple's unified memory architecture and local-first approach gives them a massive advantage as cloud AI becomes commoditized and expensive. The HN discussion debates whether this was a deliberate strategy or just luck, with many noting that local models are quickly becoming capable enough to threaten the hyperscalers' moat.

### [Taking on CUDA with ROCm: 'One Step After Another'](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47745284)
A look at AMD's efforts to compete with Nvidia's CUDA using its ROCm software stack. The HN thread is highly skeptical, pointing out ROCm's poor support for consumer cards, buggy Linux drivers, and the lack of a cohesive polyglot ecosystem. A deleted comment from an AMD employee highlighted internal bureaucracy as a major hurdle.

### [Android now stops you sharing your location in photos](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47750669)
Android now strips EXIF location data when sharing photos via web browsers or Bluetooth, requiring a USB cable to keep it intact. The HN community is split: some praise it as a privacy win for average users, while others criticize the loss of user control and the breaking of legitimate use cases like civic reporting apps.
