## Top Stories

### [Microsoft Office 2019 and 2021 for Mac view-only conversion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48341578)
Microsoft is disabling the ability to save files in perpetual-license versions of Office 2019 and 2021 for Mac due to an expiring certificate, effectively turning them into view-only software. The HN community is furious, viewing this as a breach of contract and theft. Many point out the irony that pirated versions will continue to work perfectly, while paying customers are punished.

### [Domain expertise has always been the real moat](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48340411)
A blog post arguing that while AI makes coding cheaper, the real value lies in domain expertise—knowing what the correct output should look like. The comments feature a sharp debate. The best take notes that successful software requires the intersection of both domain knowledge and software engineering discipline, and AI currently lacks the latter's rigor for edge cases.

### [OpenRouter raises $113M Series B](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48338660)
OpenRouter, an API proxy that routes requests to various LLMs, raised $113M at a reported $1.3B valuation. HN is highly skeptical of the valuation for what is essentially a proxy layer. However, power users defend the service for its unified billing, rate limits, and zero-friction model switching. The founder chimed in to explain the raise is for balance sheet durability to handle massive token spend commitments.
