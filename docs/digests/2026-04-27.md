### [I bought Friendster for $30k – Here's what I'm doing with it](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47914165)
The Friendster domain has been resurrected as an iOS app with a unique constraint: you can only add friends by physically tapping phones. The creator wants to enforce real-life connections. HN is split between praising the anti-scale, high-friction design and criticizing the iOS-only limitation.

### [SWE-bench Verified no longer measures frontier coding capabilities](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47910388)
OpenAI is moving away from the popular SWE-bench coding benchmark, revealing that 59% of the audited problems models failed had flawed test cases. The HN discussion highlights the inevitable lifecycle of AI benchmarks: they are created, models overfit or game them (Goodhart's Law), and they become obsolete.

### [Fast16: High-precision software sabotage 5 years before Stuxnet](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47913855)
A deep dive into a newly analyzed piece of malware that subtly corrupted math in scientific software, predating Stuxnet. HN commenters were fascinated by the malware's use of ancient source control notation (RCS) in the mid-2000s, a strong indicator of government or military origins.
