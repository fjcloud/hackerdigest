## Top Stories

### [An OpenAI model has disproved a central conjecture in discrete geometry](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48212493)
OpenAI used a general-purpose reasoning model to find a counterexample to a longstanding Erdős conjecture in discrete geometry. The model generated a 125-page chain-of-thought to arrive at the proof. While some commenters debated whether this is true mathematical discovery or just advanced pattern matching, the consensus is that AI is rapidly becoming an indispensable tool for exploring complex mathematical search spaces.

### [GitHub confirms breach of 3,800 repos via malicious VSCode extension](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48207660)
GitHub confirmed that an employee's device was compromised by a poisoned VSCode extension (widely rumored to be Nx Console), leading to the exfiltration of ~3,800 internal repositories. The hackers are currently auctioning the data. The HN discussion heavily criticized VSCode's lack of extension sandboxing, noting that the editor's security model has been a known vulnerability for years.

### [SpaceX S-1](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48213933)
SpaceX filed its S-1 ahead of an IPO, revealing fascinating financials. While Starlink is a cash machine ($11.4B revenue), the company is operating at a loss due to massive capex and the xAI merger. The biggest surprise in the filing: Anthropic is paying SpaceX a staggering $1.25B per month to lease compute on the Colossus supercomputer. Commenters were stunned by the scale of the Anthropic deal and Musk's retention of 85.1% voting control.

### [Incident Report: May 19, 2026 – GCP Account Suspension](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48204770)
Railway published their post-mortem on the abrupt suspension of their Google Cloud account. The outage was caused by a platform-wide automated action by GCP that affected multiple accounts without any prior warning or human review. The incident has terrified the HN community, reinforcing the long-standing fear that building a business on GCP means risking sudden death by an opaque, automated algorithm.
