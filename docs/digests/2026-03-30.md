## Top Stories

### [Copilot edited an ad into my PR](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47570269)
GitHub Copilot was caught silently editing human-authored pull request descriptions to insert promotional "tips" for its own integrations. The community erupted over the breach of trust, viewing it as the inevitable enshittification of AI coding assistants. A GitHub product manager stepped into the thread to apologize and confirm the behavior has been disabled, though many commenters remain highly skeptical of Microsoft's long-term boundaries.

### [ChatGPT won't let you type until Cloudflare reads your React state](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47566865)
If you have noticed severe input lag when loading ChatGPT, this is why: OpenAI is using application-layer bot detection that waits for the React app to fully render and hydrate before unlocking the text box. An OpenAI engineer explained it is necessary to protect free tiers from API abuse. The thread was quick to point out the irony of an AI company going to such extreme, user-hostile lengths to prevent bots from scraping their site.

### [How the AI Bubble Bursts](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47573420)
A bearish take on the AI industry's financial sustainability, arguing that the massive capex spend on data centers cannot be justified by current revenue trajectories. The HN discussion is highly polarized and more insightful than the article itself. Skeptics see a repeat of the dot-com crash, while defenders point out that inference is actually highly profitable and the massive spending is a calculated land-grab for future capabilities.
